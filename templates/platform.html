Platform Overview

How Heal Hub turns hospital pressure into coordinated action

Heal Hub is designed as a lightweight command layer for health systems. It receives patient intake, scores urgency, evaluates live capacity, and recommends the safest next move across the network.

Core system loop Intake, score, prioritize, allocate, redirect, and continuously rebalance.
01Input

Capture symptoms and vitals.

02Decide

Score urgency and assign action.

03Optimize

Balance capacity across hospitals.

Clinical layer Urgency scoring built from symptoms, severity, and vital instability
Operations layer Resource allocation based on beds, doctors, ICU slots, and queue pressure
Network layer Smart redistribution when a different hospital can deliver safer or faster care

System flow

The operating model behind Heal Hub

01 Intake

Collect patient symptoms, severity, vitals, arrival mode, and risk context.

02 Score

Generate an urgency score from 0-100 and assign a triage label.

03 Prioritize

Rank cases by urgency, instability, and wait-time pressure.

04 Allocate

Match live cases to doctors, beds, and ICU slots where possible.

05 Redirect

Transfer patients when another hospital offers lower delay or better fit.

06 Optimize

Continuously rebalance the system as new arrivals and surges appear.

AI triage scoring

Explainable prioritization

Heal Hub combines rule-based scoring with operational weighting so every case gets a transparent urgency score, triage label, and supporting breakdown.

Resource allocation

Scarcity-aware treatment decisions

The platform compares available doctors, bed capacity, ICU supply, and queue pressure before placing a patient into immediate care or a monitored queue.

Redistribution engine

Network-level patient flow optimization

When a hospital is overloaded, Heal Hub searches for a better destination and explains the trigger, whether it was ICU scarcity, doctor shortage, or excessive wait time.

Surge simulation

Emergency pressure testing

Surge mode injects realistic arrival spikes so teams can watch priorities, hospital loads, and redirection decisions change in real time.
