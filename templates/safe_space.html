If something feels wrong

Trust the discomfort and slow the situation down

Pause

You do not owe anyone instant access, immediate answers, or continued conversation when you feel unsafe.

Check the pattern

Look for repeated pressure, guilt, control, secrecy, or attempts to isolate you from people you trust.

Reach out

Tell a friend, mentor, family member, counselor, or support organization what is happening and ask them to stay with you through the next step.

Safe AI use

Helpful technology should still respect your boundaries

Keep private details private

Avoid sharing passwords, financial data, or intimate content with chatbots or unfamiliar apps.

Use AI as support, not replacement

AI can be useful for reflection and information, but it should never replace safe human help when you need real support.

Ask for real help when needed

If an interaction feels manipulative, coercive, or emotionally harmful, step away and talk to a trusted person or professional.

How to ask for help

Simple words you can borrow

To a trusted friend

"Something has been making me feel unsafe or pressured. Can I talk to you about it?"

To a therapist or counselor

"I want support around boundaries, emotional safety, and understanding a situation that doesn't feel healthy."

To a support organization

"I need guidance on what my options are and what a safer next step could look like."
